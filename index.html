Mission Statement

1. To carry out high-quality research in Geoinformatics and effectively communicate that research to science and society, such as through open-source software and publications.

2. To create technologies, tools, and methods for observing, evaluating, modeling, comprehending, and communicating geospatial processes and phenomena.

3. To provide students who are proactive, highly motivated, and qualified with excellent career opportunities upon graduation.

4. To give everyone in society the ability to solve important problems related to space and location at the local, regional, and global levels (including citizens, schools, underprivileged groups, and developing nations).

5. To keep a welcoming, cooperative, and well-equipped workplace in order to motivate the school's international staff and students to reach their full potential.

6. To actively collaborate with international, national, and local partners representing a variety of fields and segments of society.
